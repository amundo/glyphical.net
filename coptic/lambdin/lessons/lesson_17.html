17.4 Transitivity^

There is a certain ambiguity surrounding the terms transitive and intransitive in classifying Coptic verbs. The strictest definition of a transitive verb requires (1) that its direct object be marked with the "preposition" ‚≤õÃÖ (‚≤ôÃÖ‚≤ô‚≤ü‚∏ó) and (2) that the general equivalence ‚≤ï‚≤±‚≤ß ‚≤ôÃÖ‚≤ô‚≤üœ• = ‚≤ï‚≤ü‚≤ßœ•ÃÖ be attested for the verb, i.e. that the verb possess prenominal and presuffixal forms. Crum in his Coptic Dictionary, the standard lexical work in the field. Verbs not satisfying either of these criteria are labeled intransitive or are left unlabeled.

In the present work the designation transitive is extended to include verbs having prenominal and presuffixal forms that correspond exactly in meaning to the infinitive with ‚≤â or ‚≤õÃÖ‚≤•‚≤Å (e.g. ‚≤•‚≤±‚≤ß‚≤ôÃÖ ‚≤â, œ£‚≤ì‚≤õ‚≤â ‚≤õÃÖ‚≤•‚≤Å). Some subjectiveness remains, however, and one can sympathize with W. E. Crum in his desire to drop the terms transitive and intransitive altogether (op. cit., p. vii).
