Coptic plurals

From قواعد اللغة المصرية القبطية. Transcription by Ahem Boi

Pr. Shaker Basilios

Translated

In masculine nouns that end with ⲟ

Their plurals turn the ⲟ into an ⲱ and adds ⲟⲩ

Ⲟⲩⲣⲟ=>Ⲟⲩⲣⲱⲟⲩ Ⲓⲁⲣⲟ=Ⲓⲁⲣⲱⲟⲩ

Exceptions being ϧⲉⲗⲗⲟ and ⲣⲁⲙⲁⲟ, being ϧⲉⲗⲗⲟⲓ and ⲣⲁⲙⲁⲟⲓ But ⲣⲁⲙⲁⲱⲟⲩ also works

Likewise with nouns that end with ⲉ

You turn ⲉ into a ⲏ and add ⲟⲩ

Ⲃⲉⲭⲉ=>Ⲃⲉⲭⲏⲟⲩ Ϣ̀ϫⲉ=>Ϣ̀ϫⲏⲟⲩ

Derived names that end with ⲉ like ⲥⲁⲃⲉ coming from ⲥⲃⲱ and ⲑⲉϣⲉ coming from ⲑⲱϣ Get pluraled by adding a ⲩ in the ending

So it is:

Ⲥⲁⲃⲉ=>Ⲥⲁⲃⲉⲩ Ϣⲁϥⲉ=>Ϣⲁϥⲉⲩ Ⲑⲉϣⲉ=>Ⲑⲉϣⲉⲩ

Irregular plural nouns that end with consonants get pluraled in 4 ways

1: Adding ⲩ to the vowel before the last consonant and turn the vowel into ⲁ if it isn’t already

ⲥ̀ⲛⲁϩ=>ⲥ̀ⲛⲁⲩϩ ⲙ̀ⲕⲁϩ=>ⲙ̀ⲕⲁⲩϩ ϭⲁⲗⲟϫ=>ϭⲁⲗⲁⲩϫ

2: if the last consonant is ⲣ and the vowel that comes before it is ⲟ, ir gets strengthened into a ⲱ, and this rule applies to some other nouns thar i think had the ⲣ in older Egyptian but they fell

Ⲟⲩϩⲟⲣ=>ⲟⲩϩⲱⲣ Ϩ̀ⲑⲟ=>Ϩ̀ⲑⲱⲣ

3: Words ending in ⲏⲧ or ⲓⲧ get pluraled into ⲁϯ as their end

Ⲃⲏⲧ=>Ⲃⲁϯ Ⲙⲉⲛⲣⲓⲧ=>Ⲙⲉⲛⲣⲁϯ Ϩⲁⲗⲏⲧ=>Ϩⲁⲗⲁϯ

4: Nouns with strong vowels aka ⲏ or ⲱ/ⲟⲩ get turned into weaker vowels ⲉ and ⲟ and get a ⲓ in their ends

Ⲁⲫⲱⲫ=>Ⲁⲫⲟⲫⲓ Ⲁⲃⲱⲕ=>Ⲁⲃⲟⲕⲓ Ϣ̀ⲫⲏⲣ=>Ϣ̀ⲫⲉⲣⲓ Ⲓⲱⲧ=>Ⲓⲟϯ

The oddities that don’t follow any of the last ones being

Ⲥⲟⲛ=>Ⲥ̀ⲛⲏⲟⲩ Ϫⲟⲓ=>Ⲉ̀ϫⲏⲟⲩ

For feminine(able) nouns:

1: Nouns that end with ⲱ or ⲏ get ⲟⲩⲓ added to them

Ⲉⲧⲫⲱ=>Ⲉⲧⲫⲱⲟⲩⲓ Ⲉⲣⲙⲏ=>Ⲉⲣⲙⲱⲟⲩⲓ (Here ⲏ was turned into ⲱ)

And that applies to some masculine(able) nouns such as:

Ⲁⲗⲟⲩ=>Ⲁⲗⲱⲟⲩⲓ Ⲣⲉϥϫⲱ=>Ⲣⲉϥϫⲱⲟⲩⲓ Ⲥⲫⲓⲣ=>Ⲥⲫⲓⲣⲱⲟⲩⲓ

But structure of the word changes

2: Feminine nouns that end with ⲉ turn the ⲉ into ⲏ and add ⲟⲩⲓ

Ⲁⲫⲉ=>Ⲁⲫⲏⲟⲩⲓ Ⲫⲉ=>Ⲫⲏⲟⲩⲓ Ϧⲣⲉ=>Ϧⲣⲏⲟⲩⲓ

And this also applies to some masculines, by changing structure

Ϩⲱⲃ=>Ϩⲃⲏⲟⲩⲓ Ⲉⲣⲫⲉⲓ=>Ⲉⲣⲫⲱⲟⲩⲓ

And last ones being irregulars to all we just said

Ⲥ̀ϩⲓⲙⲓ=>Ϩⲓⲟⲙⲓ Ⲓⲟⲙ=>Ⲁⲙⲁⲓⲟⲩ
